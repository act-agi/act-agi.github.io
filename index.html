Data-driven Toolkit to Combat Cyberbullying in Teens (DTCCT)

Doctoral project by Kanishk Verma

What is DTCCT ?

The goal of this research is to understand from the perspective of teenagers and young adults (aged 13 to 21) and develop an Artificial Intelligent (AI) online content moderation system to tackle cyberbullying. As per the European Commission Report it is important to take children's perspective into account. Hence, we need to know,

  • How cyberbullying happens on social media networks ? (for example, TikTok, Snapchat, and Instagram)
  • How some bystanders encourage bullies and some bystanders stand-up against the bully ?
  • How cyberbullying related content (images, videos, audio, comments) are spread/shared on the social media network platform ?

DTCCT is the first attempt at developing and designing AI-based online content moderation toolkit from the perspective of teenagers and young adults.

What does participating in this research mean?

You can participate in any of the following research phases to help create an effective Anti-Cyberbullying AI-driven toolkit.

  • Online Surveys: Help researchers understand how social media platforms encourage bullying behaviour
Your participation will drive the creation of a novel and innovative toolkit to tackle Cyberbullying.
