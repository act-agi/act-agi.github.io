Data-driven Toolkit to Combat Cyberbullying in Teens (DTCCT)

Doctoral project by Kanishk Verma

What is DTCCT ?

We are trying to understand how bullying happens on social media and make a computer program that can help stop it. We want to hear from teenagers and young adults like you to help us make this program. We want to know things like:

  • Is nasty or hurtful content shared on apps like TikTok, Snapchat, and Instagram?
  • How do people share hurtful videos, pictures, and comments on social media?
  • Why do some people encourage bullies or mean people and others try to stop them?

DTCCT is the first attempt at developing and designing AI-based online content moderation toolkit from the perspective of teenagers and young adults.

What does participating in this research mean?

You can participate in any of the following research phases to help create an effective Anti-Cyberbullying AI-driven toolkit.

  • Online Surveys: Help researchers understand how social media platforms encourage bullying behaviour
  • Data Annotation: Help researchers understand type of Cyberbullying by marking existing images, videos and comments.
Your participation will drive the creation of a novel and innovative toolkit to tackle Cyberbullying.

How can you participate?

If you are below 18 years,

  • We will contact a research company/youth organisation that has access to online panels and they will contact you.

If you are 18 years and above,

  • Click here to attempt the form and someone from the research team will be in touch within 24 hours.
